Navy Pier got its name from its role as a base and training center during both World Wars. During WWII, approximately 60,000 pilots and sailors trained here, including President George H.W. Bush. After the war, the pier served as a satellite campus for the University of Illinois before falling into disuse in the 1960s. The crowds eventually returned in 1990 after major renovations converted it to the tourist attraction it is today.

Look to the south. They may not look like much, but the network of buildings and walls you see are important for both commerce and recreation on one of the busiest waterways in the country: the Chicago River. Those are the walls of the Chicago Harbor Lock, the gateway to the city and one of the engineering feats that helps the river maintain its unnatural flow towards the Mississippi.
