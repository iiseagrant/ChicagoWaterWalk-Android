The open spaces, bike and walking paths, and public art that you see all around you are the result of extensive city planning and construction. If you could go back in time to the mid-1800s, you would see a swampy shoreline that sits almost right against downtown. Fast forward a few decades and you would find yourself standing near a train yard. Today, all but 4 miles of the city's 28-mile shoreline is public parkland that has been carefully positioned and designed to make it easier for people to access and enjoy the lake.
