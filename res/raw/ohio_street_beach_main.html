The Ohio Street Beach is one of 24 lining Chicago's shore. Although a much smaller man-made beach sat near here in the early 1900s, the beach you see now formed naturally after the Jardine Water Purification Plant was built in the 1960s. The plant, which juts out into Lake Michigan just south of here, stops sand from moving along the lakefront. Because it is the only one in the city facing north, Ohio Street Beach is an ideal training site for open water swimming. You could swim in deep water from here to Oak Street Beach half a mile north without ever being more than a few feet from the safety of shallow water.

On a summer day, odds are good that the beach is open and a lot of people are enjoying a day at the lake. Strong currents or high bacteria levels, though, sometimes make the water unsafe, prompting the Chicago Park District to issue swimming advisories or close the beach altogether. But thanks to a network of environmental sensing buoys, these warnings and closures are more up-to-date and accurate today than they have ever been.
