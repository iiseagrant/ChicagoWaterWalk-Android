You are at Museum Campus, home to Adler Planetarium, Shedd Aquarium, and the Field Museum of Natural History. For most of their history, these museums were divided by Lake Shore Drive. The lanes were moved in 1998 to make the site more pedestrian-friendly with green spaces, gardens, jogging paths, and walkways. Spanning 57 acres, Museum Campus attracts millions of residents and tourists each year, making it one of the most-visited locations in Chicago.

Look out into the water and you will see the southernmost point of the Chicago breakwater, which protects the downtown shoreline from heavy waves. If you look closely, you will see cracks, rust, and other signs of deterioration. Much of this is regular wear and tear from constant waves, but the corrosion has gotten worse in recent decades. Parts of the barrier that should be underwater have been exposed to air due to Lake Michigan's low water levels.
